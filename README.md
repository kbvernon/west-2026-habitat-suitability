**Title:** "One theory, many models of habitat suitability"

**Authors:** K. Blake Vernon

**Abstract**

A major area of research in ecology concerns habitat choice behavior.
Statistical models of habitat choice are commonly referred to as Species
Distribution Models (SDMs). While ecologists have devised many sophisticated
ways of fitting SDMs to data, they do not always pay the same level of attention
to theory, specifically to the Ideal Free Distribution, an optimality model
derived from Darwin's theory of natural selection. Here I spell out the
differences between statistical and optimality models of habitat choice, between
SDMs and the IFD, focusing on modes of inference and explanation. Using an old
verificationist argument, I also show that the Ideal Free Distribution has
conceptual priority over SDMs - SDMs would have no meaning without it. This
argument also suggests that there is more to the evaluation of models than their
goodness of fit, generality, and complexity.
